\section{Introduction}

Federated learning enables collaborative model training across distributed edge devices without sharing raw data. However, existing client selection mechanisms face challenges in heterogeneous environments: non-IID data distribution, limited energy resources, and insufficient quantification of individual client contributions.

This paper proposes a Shapley value and energy-aware dual scheduling mechanism with secure gradient transmission. Our main contributions are:

\begin{itemize}
\item Shapley value-based contribution assessment using MC-Shapley approximation (Monte Carlo permutation sampling) with exponential smoothing for dynamic adaptation in non-IID scenarios
\item Energy sustainability modeling through wireless channel conditions with cumulative residual energy tracking
\item Lyapunov optimization framework for dynamic weight adjustment between accuracy and energy objectives
\item AES-256-GCM authenticated encryption for gradient transmission, providing confidentiality and integrity under an honest-but-curious server threat model without accuracy degradation
\item Comprehensive evaluation on CIFAR-10 with 100 clients under extreme non-IID conditions (Dirichlet $\alpha=0.1$)
\end{itemize}
